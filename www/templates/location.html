(St. Paul's Cathedral, German Evangelical Lutheran Church)

St. Paul's Cathedral (Kirch) - the Lutheran Cathedral of St. Paul's German Evangelical Lutheran Church in Ukraine, Ukrainian Lutheran religious center of the German tradition of the Church - the historic building and architectural monument of national importance, which houses the Department of the Bishop of the whole Church in Ukraine. Main plan

1

The church porch

At the entrance under the bell tower a plaque in memory of the victims of persecution and violence is also placed. For all the victims there are presented the names of the pastor Carl Vogel and organist Theophil Richter. Both were shot during the Stalin's rule. A wooden cross is a gift by Andreas Loquai from Bavaria, and the two angels in Baroque style came from the Catholic Church in the Urals. Glass door welcomes visitors with the words "Peace be with you" in eight languages.

2

Icon

Icon of the Virgin at the entrance of the church was painted in the Western Ukraine. During the Revolution the icon was saved by an elderly woman from a burning rural church. She kept it at her home for many years. After the accident that happened to the woman’s daughter and she miraculously survived, the woman handed over the icon as a gift for the St. Paul’s Church.

Information stand about the history of the Kirch

A series of photographs and brief notes about how the Kirch was built in Odessa.
